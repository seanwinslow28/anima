# Risk Bible — sean-anchor

The Bible's negative space. What this character is and isn't authored to do, where the source references stopped, and where downstream agents should escalate rather than trust.

## What the sean-anchor Bible covers

The Bible authors against Sean's pencil-test-colored register, in full color (the anchor's real palette). The four body views — 3-quarter, profile-left, profile-right, and back — are ingested from Sean's hand-trimmed crops of the cleaner full turnaround (`source-refs/body-turnarounds/`, cropped from `source-refs/turnaround-1.png` with the A-2 production label removed). The head views are sourced from the same colored turnaround sheet: head-front, head-3quarter, and head-profile-left are colored `#region` crops of `turnaround-1.png`; head-profile-right and head-back are generated against the anchor. (The line-art head-turn frames stay in `motion_plates/` where they belong — the register inconsistency fixed 2026-05-29.) The 9-frame head-turn sequence is preserved as a complete motion plate set. The walk-cycle source line-art is preserved as a single canonical proportion + stride reference; the derived stylistic experiments in `source-refs/walk-cycle/` are not promoted into the Bible as authoritative motion plates.

The expression set is interpretive and minimal — four states (neutral, focused, surprised, contemplative), all generated by NB Pro against the anchor plus the head-front plate. Cy authored these because the source references didn't carry a discrete emotion library; the four states cover the emotional range visible across Act 1's shipped frames plus the immediate Act 2 needs.

The costume covers the default outfit only: navy tee (#243044), cool-gray jeans (#5C6471), warm-gray sneakers (#7E807B), and the stylus. No costume variants exist. The palette is full color but limited and desaturated — cream paper (#F2E6CC), warm graphite line (#3D3530), warm skin (#F0DFCB), dirty-blonde hair (#AD9468), navy tee, cool-gray jeans, sneakers, and blue eyes (#4A6D8C). Full color is canonical Sean; the earlier four-step monochrome-graphite spec was the visual-fidelity bug fixed 2026-05-29.

## What the sean-anchor Bible does not cover

The back-of-skull silhouette is extrapolated from the cleaner turnaround sheet plus the head-turn sequence; neither reference draws the back angle from a true back-camera position. The generated head-back plate is Cy's best interpretation, not a direct observation. A Phase 5 hero shot that frames a tight back-of-head view will likely surface a Bible silence rather than a Bible violation — Em should escalate to Sean rather than fail the frame against a rule the Bible can't honestly defend.

Motion plates beyond the walk-cycle source and the head-turn 9-frame sequence are absent. Turn-in-place, run, jump, sit-down, stand-up, reach, throw, gestural variants for the right hand outside the stylus grip — none of these are authored. A Phase 6 motion clip needing any of these requires either a new plate authoring pass via `pipeline bible iterate --target motion_plates` or an explicit Sean-approved out-of-Bible call. The derived walk-cycle plates in source-refs are documented as stylistic experiments and are not authoritative for IR.sean.motion.* rule citation; the source line-art is.

The expression set is four states. Anger, confusion, frustration, delight, laughter, exhaustion, neutral-with-stylus-active are all absent. Em should treat a verdict citing an expression outside the authored four as a Bible silence (no rule to violate) rather than a Bible failure. Sean can extend the set via `pipeline bible iterate --target expressions --add <name>,<name>` without re-running the full Bible.

The palette is the anchor's full-color set rendered in the pencil-test register (flat color fills with warm-graphite cross-hatched shadow, never gradient- or cel-shaded). If a future portfolio piece needs Sean rendered in a different register — under candlelight, in a screwball-comedy palette, as a watercolor wash, in a digital animation finish — that is a style register escalation requiring a separate Bible variant, not a re-tint of this one. The pencil-test-colored register is brittle by design; the brittleness is the aesthetic.

The 3D mannequin references in `source-refs/3d-mannequin/` are useful as pose-vocabulary scaffolding only. They do not carry Sean's face, hair, or palette and were not used to anchor any IR.sean.* identity rule in this commit. Cy treated them as informational, not authoritative.

## Where downstream agents should escalate

When Em flags a Phase 5 frame for identity drift, the chairman reads this risk-bible to decide whether the drift sits on a covered axis (real failure — block) or an uncovered axis (Bible silence — escalate to Sean for either a new plate or an explicit out-of-Bible approval). The escalation paths are:

- Back-of-skull silhouette drift: escalate. The Bible's view is interpretive, not observed.
- Expression outside the authored four: escalate. Run `pipeline bible iterate` to extend.
- Motion outside walk-cycle and head-turn: escalate. New motion plate authoring is required.
- Costume variant request: escalate. Open a new `costumes/{variant}/` directory and a separate Bible pass.
- Gradient/cel shading, or any departure from the pencil-test register's flat-color-plus-cross-hatch palette: escalate. This is a style register escalation, not a Bible mutation.

## The Act 1 contract conflict guard

The Pencil Test Act 1 has shipped (Frames 1-42 at 12fps). The approved keyframes under `runs/act1-*/approved/` are the authoritative continuity reference for what already exists in the world. Sean asked Cy to treat any IR.sean.* rule that would invalidate Act 1's shipped frames as a contract conflict to flag rather than enforce. Cy authored conservatively against the source references and the existing anchor and did not visually verify each rule against the shipped Act 1 frames during Pass 1. Before Sean runs `pipeline bible approve`, the Act 1 frames should be sampled against the locked rules and any conflicts surfaced. The most likely conflict points are the line-weight rule (Act 1 may carry slightly different weight) and the cross-hatching rule (early Act 1 frames may have been rendered with filled shadow shapes rather than hatching). If conflicts surface, the rule should be relaxed at the description level or the Act 1 frames flagged for re-render — Sean's call, not Cy's.
