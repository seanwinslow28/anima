# Risk Bible — Claude Mascot

**What the claude-mascot Bible covers and doesn't cover.**

The Bible authors against the mascot's pencil-test-colored register — the same register as sean-anchor, because the mascot rides on Sean's shoulder in Act 2 and must read in the same medium as him. Unusually for a first pass, all five canonical turnaround angles — front, three-quarter front, side, three-quarter back, and back — are covered directly by Sean's C-1 turnaround sheet and ingested at the pixel layer, not extrapolated. The angle-expansion gap the retired pixel-art mascot carried is closed at the source. What is not covered: intermediate angles between the five canonical views — a low three-quarter for a shoulder-height shot, a near-top-down for a perch framing — are interpolations a Phase 5 frame has to synthesize, and Em should treat a verdict on one of those framings as a softer call than a verdict on a canonical angle.

**Motion plates — the load-bearing gap is now CLOSED at the animatic tier (2026-05-30).** Sean drew six motion sequences by hand (idle/settle, head-body tilt, perch-settle, alert-perk, hop, sleep-settle), cropped into 22 per-pose plates (`motion_plates/{idle,look,perch,alert,hop,sleep}-NN.png`) and ingested into the locked Bible via `bible add` alongside seven new `IR.claude-mascot.motion.*` rules (content_version 1.2.0). Em can now ground a motion-arc verdict against the Bible — a motion verdict is no longer Bible silence. **Colored-pass complete (2026-05-31):** Sean's hand-colored motion frames have replaced the line roughs at the canonical `motion_plates/*.png` paths — they now carry the full pencil-test register (terracotta fill, cross-hatch, cream paper, construction line), their plate entries cite the palette/style register rules, and the earlier "a color/register verdict is expected-incomplete" caveat is **lifted**: the full register now applies to motion plates. The 22 line-rough crops are preserved as the Phase 4 animatic record at `source-refs/motion-line-roughs/`. The idle cycle gained a 4th key (`idle-04`, risen breath peak) drawn during coloring; motion plates total 23. Two poses still carry known issues that survived into color (ingested as-is by Sean's call): the `hop-*` side end-cap disc reads too large, and `alert-03`'s nubs flare arm-like — see cy-confidence-notes.md; Em should treat both as known-rough. Intermediate or additional cycles are a `bible add` away on the same path.

**Expression range is narrow by design.** Cy authored two expression plates — calm/neutral and curious — against a creature whose entire emotional read lives in two dot eyes, two short brow strokes, and head tilt. There is no mouth to emote with; that is the design, not a limitation to fix. A fuller range — alarm, delight, sleep, alert-perk — would need authoring before Em can cite expression-specific rules outside the calm/curious baseline. Treat any verdict citing an unauthored expression as Bible silence, not Bible violation. When Sean wants more states, run `pipeline bible iterate --target expressions --add alarmed,delighted,sleepy`.

**Palette and register are locked; no costumes.** The palette is locked to a terracotta-orange body on warm cream paper, warm-graphite line, and cross-hatched shadow — the identical register as sean-anchor so the two share a frame in Act 2 without a medium clash. The mascot has no costume variants; the design itself is its only outfit and no accessories (a hat, a scarf, a worn tool) are planned for commit 2. If a downstream piece needs the mascot under different light — screen-glow, candlelight, night — that is a style-register escalation to a separate Bible variant, not a palette extension here. The creature's identity is deliberately brittle: a rounded box, terracotta fill, two nubs, four legs, and a construction-line face is a small set of invariants, and that smallness is exactly what lets it read as itself on Sean's shoulder at thumbnail scale.
