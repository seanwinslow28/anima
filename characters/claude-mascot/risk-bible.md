# What the claude-mascot Bible covers and doesn't cover

The Bible authors against the Claude Mascot's pixel-art-8bit register — a closed four-step indexed palette, integer-pixel-grid alignment with no anti-aliasing, and chibi 2:3 head-to-body proportion. Plates exist for the canonical front view (the anchor, reused as `turnarounds/body-front.png` and `expressions/neutral.png` since the anchor's pose is the neutral front), plus three generated turnarounds (three-quarter, profile-right, back) and two additional expression plates (surprised, contemplative). The back turnaround is extrapolated from the front silhouette and the two secondary source-ref poses; Cy has hedged this in `cy-confidence-notes.md`, and Em should weight pose, palette, and proportion rules more heavily than fine feature placement when verdicting back-view frames.

No motion plates exist in this Bible. The mascot has no authored walk cycle, idle, head turn, or gesture set. Any Phase 6 motion shot involving the mascot requires motion-plate authoring as a prior step; the runner should treat any `IR.claude-mascot.motion.*` citation from a downstream agent as a Bible silence (rule never authored), not a Bible violation (rule violated), until those plates are added. Adding motion plates is a `pipeline bible iterate --target motion_plates` pass with a fresh round of Pass 1 → Pass 2 → Pass 3 over the new plates only — not a re-author of the whole Bible.

The expression set is minimal by design — neutral, surprised, contemplative — anchored against what the three source-ref poses cover. A full emotional range (anger, joy, confusion, frustration, delight) needs authoring before Em can cite expression-specific rules outside the authored three; until then, frames requesting those expressions escalate to Sean for an iterate-add decision. The costume set is similarly minimal: there is one outfit — the mascot's own design — with no hats, accessories, tools, or alternate variants planned at commit 2. A future variant lives at `characters/claude-mascot/costumes/{variant}/` with its own authoring pass; do not interpolate from the default.

The four-step indexed palette is brittle by design — the brittleness is the aesthetic. Any lit variant (neon, candle, moonlight, golden-hour) is a style-register escalation to a separate Bible, not a palette extension in this one. Downstream agents requesting the mascot in alternate lighting should escalate to Sean for a brief-update decision rather than interpolate within the existing palette. Likewise, no smooth-edge / high-resolution / anti-aliased render variant exists: the integer-pixel-grid constraint is structural to this register's identity, and a smoothed render would route the character to a different style register and a different Phase 5 generator entirely.
