# Good-EXPAND rubric — the six-criterion live-review checklist

**This is a live human-review checklist for Sean — not a CI gate, not a
prose-grep, not a self-assessment. A model cannot self-pass it.** Every
criterion is a taste judgment; the honesty of this instrument is that it
stays in human hands. (The red-team cut every attempt to automate it:
a volume count is gameable, a boundary lint gives false confidence.)

Divergence-side companion to `pinata-worked-example.md` (the convergence-side
quality bar). The worked positive throughout is the ai-guru live run — the
`### micro-expand (workshop continuation — risks 2 & 3)` block in its session
sidecar, which produced [L5] (the ending) and [L6] (the stakes/clock).

## The six criteria

A good EXPAND on a contested axis exhibits **all six**:

### 1. Real fork
A genuinely contested axis — a live tension the room hasn't resolved — not
one manufactured to look busy.
- **Worked positive:** Sean himself flagged the ending (risk 2: "resolves too
  neatly/saccharine") and the stakes (risk 3: "does 'wants to go viral' need
  harder stakes?") as unresolved before locking further. The room didn't
  invent the fork; it was already open.
- **Anti-example:** workshopping "what color is his shirt" to demonstrate
  divergence is happening.

### 2. Mutually distinct options
The options occupy different regions of the space (the anti-clustering
discipline), not four phrasings of one idea. *Judged by Sean, live — no CI
oracle exists for distinctness.*
- **Worked positive:** the four endings — cyclical/nihilistic (success IS the
  horror), escalation/treadmill (no lesson, next platform), permanent
  mutation (the transformations never reverse), bait-and-switch irony (the
  accidental raw moment is what goes viral) — four different structural
  mechanics, not one ending in four outfits.
- **Anti-example:** four semantic-neighbour endings that are all "he learns
  fame isn't everything," reworded.

### 3. Each option a named specific with its tradeoff
- **Worked positive:** "his biggest hit is an accidental humiliating clip he
  never meant to film — and he's mortified it worked," or permanent
  mutation's stated cost ("visual gut-punch, cruelty without malice").
- **Anti-example:** "a surprising ending," "higher stakes" — categories, not
  options; nothing to veto.

### 4. Converges
A synthesized recommendation (combining options is fair), stated so Sean
accepts or vetoes in a line.
- **Worked positive:** ending = options 1+4+2 fused (accidental humiliating
  hit as horror-success, then he immediately starts gaming "authenticity" —
  the treadmill); stakes = 1+3 fused (subscriber deadline tied to the first
  day of school + the personified algorithm). Both landed as one-line
  accept/veto calls and became [L5]/[L6].
- **Anti-example:** a list with no lean — "all four could work!" — which
  hands Sean homework instead of a decision.

### 5. Protects the fragile intuition
The duality/tension that makes the spark special survives, not collapsed
into a clean moral. **The anima-specific bar — the whole lesson of the
piñata dry-run.**
- **Worked positive:** the admiration/mockery duality (named in the
  micro-expand observations as the fragile high-value intuition). The fused
  ending keeps it: no warm authenticity lesson — he treats the real moment
  as data and starts engineering "being real." The satire stays fond.
- **Anti-example:** the duality flattened into "social media bad" — a clean
  moral the spark was specifically better than.

### 6. Surfaces buildability risks as `open_questions`
A register or mechanic anima can't yet build gets flagged, not waved
through. Flagging is the workshop's whole job here — the *handling* belongs
to a later slice.
- **Worked positive:** `90s-nicktoon-grossout` is not in the six-register
  closed vocabulary; the run carried it into both character seeds with the
  NEW flag and the doctrine pointer (the Slice-3 extension path), instead of
  silently pretending Cy could author it today.
- **Anti-example:** a workshop that recommends a photoreal route without a
  word about the pipeline having no photoreal register.

## Live validation protocol (the Checkpoint-2 semantic gate)

Fable 5 builds to structural-green plus this protocol; **Sean runs the live
grill.** The capture is his — a copied fixture cannot stand in for it.

1. **Pick a contested axis.** Either a fresh thin spark, or re-open the
   ai-guru "ending" cold (ignore [L5]; re-derive it).
2. **Run the chain:** orchestrator → micro-expand → answer **"deepen"** →
   the inline contested-axis workshop (SKILL.md Step 1). For the mid-grill
   trigger, let INTERROGATE hit a contested axis and deepen in place.
3. **Capture the sidecar** — the `### expand:<axis-slug>` block(s) plus the
   locks that followed, and `stage_provenance` carrying `expand:<axis-slug>`.
4. **Score against the six criteria above.** All six must be judged, by
   Sean, against the captured transcript — not against the model's account
   of itself.
5. **Blocking rule:** a miss on criterion **2 (distinctness)** or
   **5 (intuition-protection)** blocks — those are the two failure modes
   that make a brief go generic. Misses on the others are findings to fold,
   at Sean's call.
