# Concept doc template — the movements

`concept.md` is the human-facing, museum-worthy artifact: prose, not a form.
This template names the movements a complete concept doc plays through — what
it must *cover*, never boilerplate to fill in. Write each movement in the
session's own language; if a movement genuinely doesn't apply, drop it rather
than pad it. The piñata concept doc (`pinata-worked-example.md`) is the
worked example of every movement below.

1. **Title options** — 3–4, lead with your lean and say why it wins.
2. **Logline** — one sentence carrying protagonist, engine, and payoff. (The
   validator checks a logline exists; its quality is on you.)
3. **Register** — the tonal contract: genre collision, where comedy is
   allowed to come from, runtime + act posture, the homage named.
4. **The character + the engine** — who's in it, and the *mechanism* that
   makes the story move (the object, the reveal, the restraint rules).
   Design-bearing specifics, one bolded handle each.
5. **Structure** — acts with rough durations and the key beats inside each.
   Concrete enough that a storyboard artist could start blocking.
6. **Style + timing bible** — the numbered directives the piece lives or
   dies on, with sources when they're borrowed craft. These flow into the
   brief's non-negotiables.
7. **The money shot** — the single image/mechanic the whole piece builds
   toward, and the craft rule that makes it land.
8. **Style routes** — ≥3 render-ready route prompts (A/B/C), visually
   distinct, each one paragraph. ($0 prose until ART-VIZ ships; Route
   letters become `style_route` ids.)
9. **Objective / audience / distribution** — your read, marked as steerable.
10. **Open threads** — what a fuller session would still grill. Honest list;
    an empty one reads as "we stopped thinking," not "we finished."
