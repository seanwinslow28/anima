# Studio Brief — Two-character scene (fixture)

A hypothetical two-character piece used to exercise Maya's multi-character
continuity handling.

## What is this story about?

Two animators at adjacent desks. One draws; the other watches over the
shoulder. A 30-second exchange between them, no dialogue.

## Who is the character?

Two characters, both animators:

- **Sean** — mid-thirties, lean build, sandy-blonde hair, soft jaw, dark navy shirt.
- **Friend** — early thirties, sturdier build, dark hair, broad jaw, faded green shirt.

Both work in the same pencil-test style. Sean uses a stylus; the friend uses a graphite pencil.

## What is the tone?

Companionable, quiet. The kind of working friendship where neither needs to speak.

### What this is NOT

- Not romantic.
- Not competitive.
- Not pencil-test glossy.

## What is the format?

30-second WebM loop. One scene, no act break.

## What is the target medium?

WebM in a portfolio walkthrough.

## What is the deadline?

Hard: 2026-09-01. Soft: 2026-08-15.

## What are the non-negotiables?

- Sean's stylus stays in right hand.
- Friend's pencil stays in right hand.
- Both characters remain visually distinct — no morphing between identities.
- Cream paper texture across all frames.
- 16:9 within 2% tolerance.
