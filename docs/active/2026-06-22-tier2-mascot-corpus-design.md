# Tier-2, Slice 1 — the mascot eval corpus + baseline (converged design)

*2026-06-22. Brainstormed with Sean in Cowork; this is the ratified design for the first slice of workstream 2 (Tier-2 Em calibration). It establishes the measurement foundation that makes every later Em calibration eval-gated and safe. Build handoff: [`2026-06-22-tier2-mascot-corpus-kickoff.md`](2026-06-22-tier2-mascot-corpus-kickoff.md). The Flow generation prompts: [`prompts/eval-corpus/claude-mascot-fixture-corpus.md`](../../prompts/eval-corpus/claude-mascot-fixture-corpus.md).*

---

## Why this, why now

Tier-2 is the autonomy core: getting Em's eye to match Sean's so he can eventually stop gating every frame. Two costed runs (06-18, 06-21) handed us the gap with specifics — and they all point at the **mascot**: Em flagged the same mascot finish-register drift three times (06-21 F3/F4/F7) that Sean ships, missed the mascot leg-count entirely (06-18), and the two namespace reads even contradicted each other on the mascot.

The blocker to acting on any of it: **the Em eval corpus is 52 cases, 100% `sean`, zero mascot.** Every Em change is eval-gated — but the eval can't gate mascot work, because there are no mascot cases. So the calibration is blocked on measurement.

## The trust bar (ratified)

Tier-2's "done" is **trustworthy assistant** — Em's flags get good enough that Sean spot-checks the frames she flags instead of reviewing every frame, with **false-pass held at 0**. A real reduction in the gating burden, achievable, and the honest first step. Full hands-off autonomy and propose→apply auto-fix stay as later exits, not this workstream's bar.

## The decomposition

Tier-2 is too big for one slice — it contains corpus-building, severity calibration, a detection fix, the escalation decision, and propose→apply. So it decomposes:

- **Slice 1 (this design) — the mascot corpus + baseline.** Pure measurement; changes nothing in Em.
- **Slice 2 — the calibration.** The finish-register *severity* fix (Em over-flags mascot finish), the leg-count *detection* fix (the rule exists, make it fire), and the cross-namespace *coherence* fix (Em[sean] must not reason about the mascot). All eval-gated against Slice 1's baseline.
- **Later — the escalation decision** (Gemini-first vs wire Opus on first-pass identity reads; discrepancy B) and **propose→apply** (the autonomy exit).

Slice 1 goes first because it's both prerequisite *and* risk-free: building a corpus adds cases, it never touches Em, so it **cannot regress the frozen verdict baseline.** It's the one move that unblocks everything while changing nothing measurable.

## The corpus

A **full mirror of the proven sean six single-axis classes**, adapted to the box-creature: box-proportion (the ~1:1.2 w:h), view-correctness (the label-side trick), anatomy-count (nub/leg count — weighted to 6, since it's the exact run blind spot), palette (terracotta), construction-lines, shading-register. Paired clean/defect, single-axis, ~46 fixtures (16 clean + 30 defect) at the sean scale — directly comparable to the sean baseline.

**Fixtures are generated by Sean in Google Flow — $0 on his subscription, exactly as the sean dataset was.** The discipline that voided the *old* sean corpus (19/23 fixtures were SHA-identical Bible copies) is preserved: fixtures are generated outputs, never the references, and the contamination guard ([`tests/test_fixture_contamination.py`](../../tests/test_fixture_contamination.py)) extends to the mascot set. The generation spec — every fixture ID ↔ its verbatim Flow prompt ↔ its class ↔ its clean pair — is [`prompts/eval-corpus/claude-mascot-fixture-corpus.md`](../../prompts/eval-corpus/claude-mascot-fixture-corpus.md), mirroring the sean spec's minimal grammar (*the references carry identity; the text carries only the beat + one corruption*). The prerequisite the old sean spec flagged — a mascot turnaround — already exists in the Bible (`source-refs/turnaround-c1.png`), so we're unblocked.

**Sean ratifies every image + label** at the STOP gate — his eye is the ground truth, the same single-labeler discipline as the sean corpus (no Cohen's κ at solo scale).

## The baseline

Mirror the proven sean **G5 protocol** — reference-blind, N=5 majority vote, gemini-3.5-flash pinned (+ Opus 4.7 on escalation, exactly as Em ships) — across the labeled mascot set, producing the **mascot verdict baseline**: precision / recall / **false-pass** on the mascot defect class, segmented (performs vs motion-proper), written to its own dated trace. This becomes the gate every Slice-2 mascot change is measured against.

## Guard (load-bearing)

Strictly additive. The sean 52 cases, the sean verdict-baseline trace, and its frozen md5 (`2af75906…`) are **untouched**; nothing in `pipeline/agents/vision_critic.py` changes this slice. The mascot baseline is a separate trace, so the standing guard cannot move. Em's contract (the `cites_criteria` invariant, the verdict vocabulary) is off-limits, and ships-red discipline holds — never tune a case until Em passes it.

## Cost

Generation + labeling are **$0** (Sean in Flow, on subscription). The only spend is the **costed baseline run** — Gemini-metered, N=5 × ~46 performs-cases ≈ a few hundred Em calls, on the order of the sean G5's 247 case-runs (a few dollars). Fleet-ops-governed, Claude-Code-driven (the env-strip operator path).

## Flow of the slice

1. **(Sean, $0)** generate the ~46 fixtures in Flow against the corpus spec; ratify each image + label.
2. **(Claude Code)** ingest: build the mascot `cases.yaml` entries (the sean per-case schema, `character_id: claude-mascot`), land the fixtures under `evals/vision_critic/fixtures/`, extend the contamination guard, keep the CI-green mocked runner passing.
3. **(Claude Code, costed)** run the reference-blind N=5 baseline → write the mascot verdict-baseline trace + `last-run` segment report.
4. **Slice-1 DoD:** a Sean-ratified mascot corpus in `cases.yaml`, contamination-guarded, with a ratified mascot verdict baseline — and the sean baseline + md5 provably untouched. That unblocks Slice 2.

## What it unblocks

Once the mascot is measurable, Slice 2 — the severity calibration, the leg-count detection fix, the cross-namespace coherence fix — becomes eval-gated, safe Em work, measured against this baseline. That's where Em actually moves toward Sean's eye; this slice is what makes that movement honest.
