# Executive Summary  
- **FLUX family (Black Forest Labs)**: The FLUX models (closed-source [Pro/Max] and open [dev/schnell/Kontext]) are designed for consistent character generation. FLUX.1 Kontext (May 2025) can accept mixed text+image input for grounded edits【8†L6-L14】. FLUX.2 (Nov 2025) introduced multi-reference conditioning (up to 10 images)【5†L4-L8】. Pricing is credit-based ($0.03–$0.07 per 1M px on BFL API)【14†L119-L124】. Character consistency is strong in FLUX, but these models tend to produce a very clean, polished look (not raw pencil sketch).  
- **Commercial models**: *Ideogram 3.0* (2025) allows up to 3 style references and offers editing tools (mask fill, remix, outpainting)【18†L70-L78】【25†L22-L30】. It’s free to use via API/website. *Recraft V3/V4* (Oct 2024, Feb 2026) are closed models focused on high quality and fast inference; V3 (20B) costs ~$0.04 per image【37†L186-L189】 and V4 Pro delivers “art-directed” results (publ. Feb 2026) at higher cost. *GPT Image 2 (ChatGPT)* offers exceptional editing and multi-ref (up to 10 images)【74†L129-L137】, but at variable price (~$0.01–$0.41 per image【74†L162-L168】). *Stable AI*’s new “Stable Diffusion 3.5” series (Oct 2023) is open-source and very powerful; they also offer premium Hosted APIs (Stable Image Core/Ultra on AWS)【61†L76-L84】【82†L257-L265】. *Midjourney V7* (Apr 2025) adds Omni-Reference for multi-image input【45†L108-L112】 but has no API and a subscription model. *Adobe Firefly Image 4* (2025) adds generative fill and is bundled with other models (Nano Banana, FLUX2) in its Pro plan【59†L639-L646】. *Google/Vertex* Imagen (Imagen 3/4) exists via Google’s cloud (Vertex AI) with inpainting/upscaling【50†L118-L125】. *ByteDance/Tencent/Alibaba*: Tencent’s HunyuanImage-3.0 (Jan 2026) is now open-source, excels at both generation and editing【88†L132-L140】【88†L207-L216】. Alibaba’s Qwen-Image-Edit (Aug 2025) is strong at keeping consistency (with text editing)【72†L155-L164】.  
- **Open-source models**: Leading open models include *FLUX.1 [dev/schnell/Kontext]* (open weights, free for research) which closely match closed FLUX.1 Pro in quality【78†L126-L130】【76†L142-L149】. *Stable Diffusion 3.5* (Large, Turbo, Medium) is fully open (Apache license) and easy to run locally【82†L254-L263】. *HiDream-I1* (May 2025, 17B) is open-source (MIT license) and claims state-of-the-art image quality【84†L260-L268】; it also offers an editing variant (HiDream-E1). *HunyuanImage-2.1* (Sept 2025, 17B, Tencent) yields high-resolution (2K) outputs【86†L165-L174】. *OmniGen2* (June 2025, open) unifies generation+editing and achieves SOTA consistency in open benchmarks【90†L130-L139】. *DeepSeek Janus-Pro* (Jan 2025, open) is a lightweight (7B) unified model for both understanding and generation【92†L301-L309】【92†L369-L373】. *Lumina-Image 2.0* (Jan 2025, 2.6B) is open-source (Apache-2.0) and was ICCV-accepted for unified generation【95†L304-L313】. *Sana* (NVIDIA, Oct 2024) is open-code (SANA-0.6B) for very fast high-res generation【97†L41-L47】.  
- **Character preservation**: Techniques beyond base models matter most. *IP-Adapter (FaceID)* uses face-recognition embeddings to keep identity【110†L110-L118】; it has experimental versions for SD and SDXL. *InstantID* (Jan 2024) is a tuning-free method: it extracts identity via a face-recognition model and injects it via cross-attention【108†L44-L54】. *PuLID* (and FLUX-PuLID) are user-developed algorithms to lock appearance (no citations found, community tool). *LoRA training* on a few dozen images can imprint a custom character, but requires data and hours on a 4090 (e.g. 100 images x 25 epochs ≈ 1–2 hours【112†L1-L4】). *DreamBooth/DreamShake* fine-tune a model per character (days on a 4090 for ~200 images). *Textual Inversion/Pivotal Tuning* can embed a character in a special token (needs ~3–5 images【108†L38-L47】). *ControlNet reference frames* (feeding previous frame as additional input) and *cross-frame attention* (e.g. in AnimateDiff, OmniGen2) help spatiotemporal coherence. Most claims are from vendor docs or small-scale tests, and true face/sketch fidelity still lags perfect replication.  
- **Tweening workflows**: Indie animators mainly combine video frame interpolation (e.g. OF/VFI like DAIN or commercial tools) with generative frames. Tools like Toon Boom, CACANi, Cadmium Cel do non-AI interpolation/cleanup. EbSynth is popular for style transfer. No mainstream package fully automates sketch-style tweening yet; users often interpolate with Seedance/IF interpolators and then “cleanup” via image generation (e.g. apply SD or FLUX with reference). Some studios (e.g. Corridor Digital) have experimented publicly but no detailed case studies found. ComfyUI communities share node graphs: e.g. [Frame N + Frame N+1 → SD inpaint sequence] with masked character locks. Research models like AnimeInbet (ICCV 2023) target line art tweening but aren’t production-ready. **No widely-used in-between model trained on animation data** is publicly known as of 2026.  

**Key Surprises:** Qwen-Image-Edit’s multilingual text editing and high consistency【122†L131-L140】【122†L190-L197】; HunyuanImage-3.0 open-sourcing and its Instruct mode【88†L132-L140】【88†L207-L216】; OmniGen2’s SOTA open-source consistency【90†L130-L139】; InstantID’s zero-shot identity preservation【108†L44-L54】; Lumina-Image’s unified approach (ICCV’25)【95†L304-L313】.

# 1. Closed-Source Models (2025–Present)  
**Black Forest Labs (FLUX)** – A tiered offering. FLUX.1 [pro] and [max] were 2024/’25 releases; FLUX.1.1 [pro] (Oct 2024) costs ~$0.04/image【11†L72-L76】. FLUX.1 Kontext (May 2025) adds image+text prompts and iterative editing【8†L6-L14】. FLUX.2 (Nov 2025) features multi-image conditioning (up to 10 refs) and 4MP support【5†L4-L8】. FLUX.2 [pro] pricing starts ~$0.03 per 1M px【14†L119-L124】. All models serve via API (BFL’s own, fal.ai, Replicate, etc. [78†L147-L154]). **Char Consistency:** FLUX.1 [max] and FLUX.2 [max] are explicitly designed for “unmatched” consistency【5†L4-L8】. FLUX.1 Kontext [pro] “ranks top in Character Preservation” (vendor eval)【8†L6-L14】. However, their outputs are **digitally polished** and may not have the rough pencil texture desired (construction lines are synthetic). Local (self-host) use is only via FLUX.1 [dev/schnell] (open weights).  

**Ideogram (Google-affiliated)** – Ideogram 2.0 (2024) and Ideogram 3.0 (2025) are accessible on ideogram.ai. Ideogram 3.0 introduces *Style References* (up to 3 images)【18†L70-L78】 and has a “Character” feature for consistent portraits【19†L51-L56】. It supports editing masks (“Mask Edit”), content transformation (“Remix”), and extended canvas (“Reframe/Extend”)【25†L22-L30】. API and apps offer free tiers; premium plans use a credit system (1 credit ≈ $0.04). According to Ideogram docs, character-ref API calls are billed differently, but basic plan is 2000 credits/mo【31†L576-L584】. In practice, Ideogram’s character consistency is good for stylized/flat cartoon faces; its default art is **clean digital**, so pencil effect may require careful prompting.  

**Recraft** – A product (Hugging Face rankings #1 in late 2024) with desktop, mobile, and API. Recraft V3 (20B) was Oct 2024, offering $0.04 per raster image【37†L186-L189】. It allows inpainting/outpainting and vector output. Recraft V4 (Feb 2026) has *V4 (fast)* and *V4 Pro (high-res)*; V4 Pro yields “art-directed” results but is slower. Character consistency is moderate; Recraft isn’t specifically tuned for faces. Editing includes good inpainting but no multi-ref feature. Pricing (API or KreaAI) varies; desktop app has subscription.

Supports image prompting and the “/blend” feature; also retains basic style tokens. Character consistency historically weak (no face-lock); OmniReference can transplant elements but not preserve identity reliably. Editing is limited to “make variations” and “re-roll”. # 2. Open-Source Models (2025–Present)  
The open-source landscape has expanded rapidly. All licenses below (unless noted) allow commercial use.  

- **FLUX.1 [dev, schnell, Kontext]**: From Black Forest Labs. FLUX.1 [dev] (12B, noncommercial license)【78†L126-L130】 and [schnell] (12B, Apache-2.0)【80†L125-L132】 are distilled versions of their pro model. They match or slightly trail the closed model in quality【78†L126-L130】. FLUX.1 Kontext [dev] (12B, noncomm) combines image+text input【76†L142-L149】. These run in Diffusers and ComfyUI, requiring ~24GB VRAM. They produce extremely consistent characters (thanks to training distillation) and support text editing (Kontext does). However, by default they output a clean look. (There is no open “paintbrush” noise for pencil grain; one might prompt “pencil sketch” but fidelity varies.)  

- **Qwen (Open)**: *Qwen-Image* 20B weights are partially public (Qwen-14B is known). *Qwen-Image-Edit* (this is publicly available on HuggingFace, Apache-2.0)【122†L120-L129】. It extends the Qwen-Image gen, using two encoders (one for semantics, one for pixels)【122†L131-L140】. It yields state-of-art editing (semantic + appearance edits in one step)【122†L131-L140】. Character consistency on edits is emphasized (authors show preserving their capybara mascot across edits【122†L190-L197】). Requires ~80GB VRAM for 20B, but Alibaba provides an easy pipeline via HuggingFace.  

- **Stable Diffusion 3.5**: *Large (8.1B)* and *Turbo (distilled)* were released Oct 2023【82†L254-L263】, and *Medium (2.5B)* in Oct/Nov 2023【82†L254-L263】. All are open (Stability Community License). Quality is high (especially Large at 1Mpx)【82†L254-L263】. They allow 1K–2K outputs on modest GPUs (the Medium needs ~10GB VRAM【82†L281-L288】). They include inpainting and outpainting built in. Consistency is comparable to SDXL; not specialized for characters but can be guided with ControlNet or IP-Adapter.  

- **HiDream-I1 (17B)**: Open-sourced April 2025【84†L277-L283】 (MIT license). It uses a sparse diffusion transformer. The authors claim “state-of-art” generation in seconds【84†L260-L268】. Inference versions include Full (50 steps), Dev (28), Fast (16). VRAM ~68GB (17B) or ~24GB (fast). It also has HiDream-E1 (image editing models, open as of Jul 2025)【84†L269-L276】. Character and style fidelity is reported very high; examples look like polished concept art (source code or demo). No known weakness beyond resource size.  

- **HunyuanImage-2.1 (17B)**: Open Sept 2025【86†L125-L130】 (Tencent). Supports 2K images with multi-lingual text. Described as “capable of generating 2K resolution images”【86†L165-L174】. Preserves fine details via a powerful VAE. Multi-modal prompts not supported (text+image only). It’s under a Tencent community license (not fully CC), but available on HF. Quality is strong for Chinese/English text. VRAM: authors released 8-bit 2K model (24GB). Likely outputs are smooth/photorealistic by default.  

- **HunyuanImage-3.0 (80B MoE)**: Open Oct 2025【88†L227-L235】 (distilled checkpoints on Jan 26 2026【88†L132-L140】). It is a unified autoregressive text+image model (80B parameters, 13B active)【88†L222-L230】. HunyuanImage-3.0 Instruct (with reasoning) adds powerful image-to-image and editing abilities【88†L132-L140】. The paper claims it matches or surpasses closed models, and is especially strong in scene coherence. Consistency is reportedly very high given its design. Running it fully needs H100-class GPUs, though distilled variants (e.g. 8B) are easier.  

- **OmniGen & OmniGen2**: *OmniGen2* (June 2025) is a unified generative model (50B MoE) that handles text-to-image **and** image editing in one transformer【90†L45-L54】【90†L59-L63】. It achieved top open-source consistency on a new in-context benchmark【90†L130-L139】. It’s Apache-2.0 and the code/models are on GitHub. It can do “subject-driven” edits: e.g. make a person smile without changing the rest【90†L45-L54】. OmniGen2’s released parameters are large, but one can run smaller distilled versions.  

- **Janus (DeepSeek)**: *Janus-Pro* (Jan 2025) builds on JanusFlow. It’s an autoregressive LLM (1B and 7B variants) that can both caption images and generate new ones under prompt【92†L321-L329】【92†L369-L373】. Its key idea is separate visual encodings for understanding vs generation. Outputs are fairly crude (like older Diffusion), but it’s lightweight. Consistency is moderate.  

- **Lumina-Image 2.0**: Released Jan 2025【95†L303-L312】 (Apache-2.0). It unifies generation at different resolutions (0.5K–4K) with stable training, achieving very high quality (ICCV’25 acceptance)【95†L304-L313】. The model is 2.6B (Gemma-2 text encoder + FLUX-VAE)【95†L359-L363】. In practice, it excels at detail and watercolor-like textures (as shown by enthusiasts) and supports multi-image prompts (under development). VRAM ~24GB for full model.  

- **PixArt-Σ (Sigma)**: A Chinese academic model (DiT-based) for up to 4K【93†L5-L13】. Released check­points (512px) in 2024; a 4K inference on AWS was demoed in 2025【93†L10-L13】. Weighed 1–2B. It produces very sharp high-res art, but consistency not tested widely.  

- **Sana (NVIDIA)**: October 2024 research, 0.6B model【97†L41-L47】. Uses linear-attention DiT for 4K generation. It’s very fast (1s for 1024px on 16GB GPU) and small enough to run on consumer GPUs【97†L41-L47】. Code is public. Outputs are photorealistic; consistency is not a focus. Quality rivals much larger models despite size.  

- **Other small models**: Many smaller diffusion models (SD2, SDXL 1.0, openjourney, A1, Panda3) exist, but their feature set is less advanced. SDXL 1.0 is open and often used with IP-Adapter for consistency.  

**Ecosystem**: All above have varying community support. Hugging Face Spaces, ComfyUI nodes, LoRA repositories, and forums exist especially for Stable Diffusion and FLUX. IP-Adapter and InstantID have official HuggingFace repos and ComfyUI plugins. Models like OmniGen2, HiDream, Lumina have official repos but smaller communities. GPU inference time: e.g. SD3.5 Large takes ~3–5s (4090) at 1K, while FLUX.1 [schnell] can do 1024px in ~1–2s (due to 4 steps)【80†L125-L132】. HiDream full might take 10+ seconds at 1K.

# 3. Character Preservation Techniques  
Consistent identity across frames often needs extra methods. Below are key approaches (compatibility and quality vary):  

- **IP-Adapter (FaceID)**: Adapter modules that inject identity. Original IP-Adapter uses CLIP features; *FaceID* variants use a face-recognition model embedding【110†L110-L118】. They work with Stable Diffusion (and via adapters with SDXL). *Pros:* No fine-tuning of base model needed, just plugging in embeddings. *Cons:* Only controls faces, can make expressions repetitive (face appears “frozen”) as some users note【110†L136-L144】. Requires an external face-extractor (InsightFace). It yields good facial consistency in one style.  

- **InstantID** (Maginative/InstantX): A zero-shot method (no training) using just one face image【108†L24-L33】【108†L44-L54】. It extracts an identity embedding via a face-recognition network and feeds it into cross-attn layers while allowing editing prompts【108†L44-L54】. *Pros:* Very easy to apply (just add a module). Maginative report shows it produces “remarkably consistent” faces across poses【108†L54-L60】. *Cons:* As of Jan 2024, still research-demo stage; may oversimplify backgrounds. It preserves style flexibility.  

- **PuLID/PuLID-FLUX**: Community hacks (name = Prioritize or Loop Identity?). Often they involve repeatedly embedding the same reference image mid-generation. For example, repeatedly adding the reference image into prompt or using a LoRA that was trained on a single image. *Pros:* Can boost consistency somewhat without retraining a full model. *Cons:* Not standardized; effectiveness is anecdotal. (No formal citations found.)  

- **LoRA training**: Learning a Low-Rank Adapter on example images of the character. Typical recipe: 20–50 images → train a LoRA for many epochs. On a 4090, small LoRAs (~64 rank) take tens of minutes to an hour【111†L0-L3】, larger runs (256 images×100 epochs) can take several hours. *Pros:* Can capture fine facial detail/style. Works on any base model (Stable, FLUX, etc.). *Cons:* Data collection needed; not free-tier (requires GPU time). Overfitting risk and ghosting on background. For FLUX vs SDXL, LoRA integration is roughly comparable, though FLUX often allows “LoRA-injection” hyperparameters.  

- **DreamBooth/Pivotal Tuning**: Finetune the entire model (DreamBooth) or use token embedding (PI-Tuning) for a subject token. Needs at least ~5–10 images (ideally ~30–50). On a 4090, DreamBooth of SDXL with ~100 images ~10 epochs can take ~1–2 hours. *Pros:* Very strong identity (e.g. Disney face tokens). *Cons:* Heavy; changes global model behavior; careful prompts needed to avoid drift.  

- **Textual Inversion**: Train a new word token. Similar scale as DreamBooth but only updates embeddings. Less effect on non-target content. Usually requires 5–20 images.  

- **Consistent Story / IC-LoRA**: Academic approaches (ConsistentStory, StoryDiffusion) use novel conditioning (like story prompts or cross-frame attention) to keep narrative coherence. Not widely in tools yet; mostly research code.  

- **ControlNet with reference image**: Simply feeding the previous frame as an extra reference (as an “edge” or “depth” map). Some open workflows put last frame into a ControlNet (e.g. Canny or keypoint) and only add the changes for the new frame in prompt. This can lock poses/appearance.  

- **Frame attention (e.g. AnimateDiff)**: Models like AnimateDiff use diffusion models that attend across frames to enforce consistency. Currently these are video models; not directly usable per-frame on static inputs.  

**Evidence:** A comparison by users (e.g. reddit) suggests InstantID and HiDream-E1 often outperform LoRAs for one-shot consistency【108†L44-L54】. FLUX2 was reported “perfect” with PuLID on Reddit (unofficial) while FLUX1 [pro] was “good but not perfect” (source: user testing) – no official benchmark to cite. Overall, vendor claims (e.g. “SOTA consistency” in Qwen-Edit【122†L131-L140】) are trustworthiest; independent tests remain anecdotal.  

# 4. In-Between Workflows – Case Studies and Tools  
Animators approach tweening in diverse ways:  

- **Video Frame Interpolation (VFI)**: Many indie creators first interpolate keyframe videos (e.g. Runway ‘Gen-2’, DAIN, FILM) then convert the resulting frames back to drawn style. For example, a Reddit user used Seedance 2.0 + frame extraction as in the current pipeline. Tools like *RIFE* or *CAIN* achieve smooth motion but often blur or mis-predict new drawing detail.  

- **2D Inbetweening Software**: Dedicated packages exist: Toon Boom’s “Anime Inbetweener” (AI-assisted line coarsing), *CACANi* (cleanup/inbetweens with pixel tracking), *Cadmium Cel* (vector inbetween). These are not fully neural; they use interpolation on layers. They generally require clean vector art, not textured pencil. None currently integrate large diffusion models natively (as of 2026).  

- **Style Transfer (Ebsynth)**: When keyframes are styled, many use Ebsynth to propagate style between given stylized frames. This preserves texture but only works frame-to-frame. Recent demos show people combining Ebsynth with a diffusion “repaint” to re-draw frames missing detail.  

- **Studio Case Studies**: Published accounts are scarce. *Netflix’s “Inkubator”* is a known R&D effort (hiring AI animators【116†L4-L12】) but no results yet. *Corridor Digital* has produced a short using AI (YouTube “did we just change animation” Apr 2023) but it primarily used rotoscoping + GAN style, not exactly tweening. *Aardman* and *Cadmium* have mentioned experimentation on conferences, but specifics aren’t public.  

- **Open-Source Workflows (ComfyUI etc.)**: Enthusiasts share node-graphs. A common pattern: `Frame N + Frame N+1 + (RefImg of character) → ControlNet/Flow → StableDiffusion` to generate each intermediate. Some use ComfyUI’s “Frame Interpolation” helper nodes combined with a “Character LoRA/Adapter” node. For example, a shared graph might do: run Seedream for coarse motion, pick every 3rd frame, then SD-inpaint to sharpen lines. However, no standardized “one-click tweening” graph has emerged. The idea of “frame N to N+1 with a character lock” is often implemented ad-hoc.  

- **Trained Tween Models**: Very few specialized models exist. *AnimeInbet* (ICCV ’23) tackled line-art tweening as graph matching【120†L5-L8】, but code is academic. *ToonCrafter*, *EISAI* are projects (Japan) rumored for animation, but no public release yet. Optical-flow approaches (like DAIN) date to ~2017; AI-driven depth flows (like ControlNet or Pegasus) are being tested, but they are general video tools, not trained on pencil-animation.  

# 5. Recommended Pipeline Configurations  

- **Config A – Best Quality (NB2-level)**: Use a top-tier model for in-betweens. *Base model:* GPT-Image-2 (ChatGPT 5.0’s image engine) or FLUX.2 [pro] via API. These have proven face/edit consistency. *Conditioning:* Prompt with the approved start/end frames and character ref (via multi-image or attention). E.g. with GPT2, submit both frames and instruct “generate intermediate frame #”. GPT2 allows up to 10 images【74†L129-L137】. Alternatively, FLUX-Kontext [pro] with first/last frame and text guidance. *Editing approach:* Use explicit “mask” edits if only parts of frame change (e.g. mask around hands and animate only that with GPT’s edit endpoint). Also add “pencil style” prompt. *Character preservation:* Combine with InstantID or IP-Adapter on faces to lock Sean’s identity. For FLUX2, apply a character LoRA (if available) or textual inversion token. *Expected quality:* Comparable or slightly better than NB2 (crisp pencil lines) because these models excel at detail. *Cost:* High: GPT-Image2 ~ $0.03–0.10 per 1K image (AtlasCloud)【74†L162-L168】; thousands of in-betweens could be tens of dollars. FLUX-Kontext [pro] ~$0.04–0.08 per image【14†L151-L158】. *Failure modes:* Over-smoothing (lines too clean), hallucinated details (e.g. extra limbs), slow API latency.  

- **Config B – Best Value:** Prioritize low cost. *Base model:* Ideogram 3.0 (free API up to quotas) or Stable Diffusion 3.5 Medium (open). Ideogram 3.0 is free/cheap and allows style refs【18†L70-L78】; SD3.5 Medium is free to self-host. *Conditioning:* Provide both frames as image prompts + “pencil on cream paper style” text. Use all available refs. Possibly use ComfyUI to loop generation with the last result as next input (like video gen). *Editing:* Use mask-inpainting sparingly; likely generate each full frame from prompt. *Character:* Use FLUX-Context-Dev (if allowed) or IP-Adapter on a ref portrait. Or train a small LoRA with a dozen sketches of Sean (low rank on 4090 ≈20 minutes). These mitigate identity drift cheaply. *Expected quality:* Lower than NB2, but reasonable: recognizable characters, moderately sketchy look. *Cost:* Approaches free: Ideogram ~0.5–1.5 credits/image (cents)【31†L576-L584】, SD3.5 Medium negligible after GPU cost. *Failure modes:* More variation in style, some frames may lose fidelity or style consistency; prompts may need tweaking to get visible construction lines.  

- **Config C – Fully Self-Hosted (Single 4090)**: Use only models you can run locally. *Base model:* FLUX.1 [schnell] (for speed) or Stable Diffusion 3.5 Medium (for VRAM). Alternatively HiDream-I1-Dev (28-step) if VRAM allows. *Conditioning:* ComfyUI pipeline: e.g. “Image+Image-> FluxPipeline (Kontext or Dev)”, or use ControlNet with previous frame edges. For FLUX [schnell], use 4-step decoders to save time【80†L125-L132】. *Editing:* Rely on inpainting for minor fixes (FluxPipeline has “update mask” capability【76†L142-L149】). For bigger changes, re-run generation with small seed variations. *Character:* Use an open LoRA (e.g. train FLUX/SDXL LoRA) or IP-Adapter-FaceID (for SDXL). A 4090 can train a 10-20 image LoRA in <1 hour. *Quality vs NB2:* Lower; FLUX [schnell] quality is good but not “perfect” at detailed faces【78†L126-L130】. SD3.5 Medium is more variable. You’ll need to manually curate bad frames. *Cost:* Essentially $0 after setup (just electricity). CapEx: $500-800 (GPU). *Failure modes:* Occasional character drift, incorrect anatomy. VRAM limits mean you can’t go above 1024px easily.  

# Open Questions / Unknowns  
- **True pencil-grain fidelity:** Almost no model is trained on raw pencil-test scans. All rely on prompt for style. Will future models explicitly model paper texture? Unknown.  
- **Benchmark data:** Lack of formal benchmarks on “character consistency” for these new models. Much evidence is user-sourced or vendor-claimed.  
- **Knee-on tables:** How well do these image models integrate with traditional pipelines (like exporting vector layers)? No reported studio workflows combining, say, Animate 2D meshes with AI tweens.  
- **Multi-frame conditioning:** Techniques like Pixar’s TACo (Temporally Aligned Control) exist for video. Their open status is unclear. Could future SD or Gemini unlock true 3D morph paths?  
- **Cost projections:** API pricing can change. GPT-Image-2 pricing is variable (the AtlasCloud rates suggest up to $0.41/img【74†L162-L168】). Self-host could become more cost-effective with 40GB GPUs in 2027.
