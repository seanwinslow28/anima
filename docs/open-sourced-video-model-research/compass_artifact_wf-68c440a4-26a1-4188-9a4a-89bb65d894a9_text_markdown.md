# Replacing Veo/Kling/Seedance locally with a 4090: what actually works in April 2026

**Short answer: yes, you can replace the paid APIs for your specific pipeline — start+end frame I2V of hand-drawn pencil frames — but not for photoreal quality parity.** The single strongest match to your use case is **Wan 2.2 FLF2V (14B MoE, FP8 + Lightning LoRA) driven by ComfyUI's native `WanFirstLastFrameToVideo` node**, with **LTX-Video 0.9.7/0.9.8 13B distilled** as a fast sibling and, critically, **AnimateDiff-Evolved + Banodoco Steerable Motion (SD1.5 + SparseCtrl + Lineart ControlNet)** as the real aesthetic-preservation winner for pencil-test work. Community blind-arena data (Artificial Analysis I2V leaderboard, 907k+ votes as of April 16, 2026) shows the best open-weights model (LTX-2 Pro, Elo 1,196) still trails Seedance 2.0 (1,352), Kling 3.0 Omni (1,298) and Veo 3.1 (1,246) by roughly 50–160 Elo — a visible drop on photoreal motion, but much smaller for stylized content where photoreal texture isn't the goal. Every top-5 candidate below has Apache-2.0 or near-open weights, native or mature ComfyUI support, and fits your 24 GB 4090 with quantization; Apple Silicon is mostly impractical except for LTX-Video and tiny Wan TI2V-5B jobs.

Two things to internalize before you pick a model: **(1) the frontier APIs still win on raw photoreal quality, so the "replacement" pitch only works because your pipeline is stylized** — pencil test on cream paper with cross-hatching is a domain where closed-model advantages shrink. **(2) First-and-last-frame (FLF2V) is the only bit where open beats closed** — Wan 2.2 has official native nodes, and AnimateDiff via SparseCtrl has been doing keyframe travel for two years. Veo/Kling/Seedance still treat end-frame control as a secondary feature or charge heavily for it.

## Section 2 — Deep dive per top 5

### 1. Wan 2.2 FLF2V (I2V-A14B used via native node)

**Overview.** Released July 28, 2025 by Alibaba as the first open-source video model with a **Mixture-of-Experts DiT** — two 14 B expert denoisers (high-noise for layout, low-noise for detail, 27 B total / 14 B active per step, ~50% compute savings). ComfyUI added native FLF2V support on August 2, 2025 — the Wan 2.2 I2V-A14B weights are re-wired through the `WanFirstLastFrameToVideo` node rather than released as a separate "Wan 2.2 FLF2V" checkpoint. Apache 2.0. Official announcement: alibabacloud.com/en/press-room/alibaba-releases-wan2-2-to-uplift-cinematic; GitHub: github.com/Wan-Video/Wan2.2; day-0 ComfyUI blog: blog.comfy.org/p/wan22-flf2v-comfyui-native-support.

**Quality vs Veo 3.1 / Kling 2.x / Seedance 2.0.** On Artificial Analysis I2V arena (Apr 2026), Wan 2.2 A14B sits at **Elo 1,119**, roughly **233 Elo below Seedance 2.0 (1,352)** and **127 Elo below Veo 3.1 (1,246)** — at this delta, frontier APIs win ~70% of blind photoreal comparisons. Strength: **FLF2V is genuinely first-class** — no closed API gives you the same keyframe control with equivalent prompt adherence (Veo 3.1 added interpolation late, Kling 2.x treats it as a power-user feature, Seedance 2.0 is best-in-class for motion but expensive per call). Weakness: **cinematic gloss, natural audio, and fire/water/crowd physics** — Pulze's side-by-side tests explicitly called Wan 2.2 "inconsistent or unrealistic" for flame simulation. The 302.AI review concluded Wan 2.2 is "decent, [with] open source its biggest selling point." Native audio is absent in the open Wan; Alibaba gated audio behind the closed Wan 2.5+ API.

**Community reception.** Three high-signal sources: (a) ComfyUI Aug 2, 2025 FLF2V blog comments and linked Reddit summary — "seamless long-form video generation at 1280×720 over 20 seconds" cited at 2,100+ upvotes per MindStudio's Reddit aggregation (flag: paraphrased, original Reddit thread could not be verified directly). (b) GitHub Issue kijai/ComfyUI-WanVideoWrapper#1721 (Dec 7 2025, user zwukong): *"Comfyui have WanFirstLastFrameToVideo node, but lack of multi frames control. Your wrapper seems lack of wan2.2 FLF2V, i only found a wan2.1 FLF2V."* Confirms: for Wan 2.2 FLF2V specifically, **use ComfyUI native nodes, not the Kijai wrapper**. (c) Stable-diffusion-art.com Oct 5, 2025 practical tutorial reports full-step Wan 2.2 FLF2V at 720p takes **"26 minutes on an RTX 4090"** without the Lightning LoRA — with Lightx2v 4-step LoRA v1.5/v2, the same clip drops to **3–6 minutes**.

**ComfyUI setup.** Native (recommended for Wan 2.2 FLF2V). Update ComfyUI to ≥0.3.39, open Workflow → Browse Templates → Video → "Wan 2.2 FLF2V." Download: `wan2.2_i2v_high_noise_14B_fp8_scaled.safetensors` and `wan2.2_i2v_low_noise_14B_fp8_scaled.safetensors` from huggingface.co/Kijai/WanVideo_comfy_fp8_scaled; `umt5_xxl_fp8_e4m3fn_scaled.safetensors`; `wan_2.1_vae.safetensors`; Lightning 4-step LoRA pair (v1.5 or v2 — Kijai notes v1 flashes at full strength, v2 "retains motion better"). For lower VRAM, swap in QuantStack's GGUF Q5_K_M / Q6_K (huggingface.co/QuantStack/Wan2.2-I2V-A14B-GGUF, 180k+ monthly downloads). Docs walkthrough: docs.comfy.org/tutorials/video/wan/wan2_2. Reference workflow JSON: RunComfy's Wan 2.2 FLF2V build at runcomfy.com/comfyui-workflows/wan-2-2-flf2v-first-last-frame-video-generation.

**Hardware reality.** **FP16 14B will not fit 24 GB at 720p** (InstaSD benchmark: "24 GB GPUs could not generate 720P videos" unquantized). **FP8 scaled (Kijai) + BlockSwap 20/40 + Lightning LoRA** is the proven recipe: ~14–18 GB peak, 3–6 min per 5 s clip on the 4090. GGUF Q5_K_M (~12 GB) leaves headroom for ControlNet or a style LoRA stack. The **Wan 2.2 TI2V-5B** variant is explicitly marketed by Alibaba for sub-24 GB cards at 720p × 24 fps in "several minutes" and is the only Wan variant realistically usable on Apple Silicon (HighDoping/Wan2.2-Mac reports ~1h37m for 720p × 41 frames on an M4 32 GB — functional but not iteration-speed). **A14B on Mac is effectively impractical.**

**I2V with end-frame support.** ✅ Native via `WanFirstLastFrameToVideo`. Also via `alibaba-pai/Wan2.1-Fun-14B-InP` (trained specifically for start+end inpainting, different motion prior — worth A/B testing on your frames). Kijai wrapper supports Wan 2.1 FLF2V but **not yet Wan 2.2 FLF2V** as of Dec 2025.

**Known limitations.** Trained heavily on cinematic/photoreal data — **expects to smooth pencil hatching toward cel/toon shading unless directed otherwise**. Mitigations: stack a 2D-anime LoRA (e.g., civitai.com/models/2222779 "Anime Style [WAN 2.2 I2V]," trigger `An1meStyl3`, author explicitly calls out Wan 2.2's realism bias) at 1.0–1.2, heavy negative prompt (`((realistic)), ((photograph)), motion blur, smooth shading`). **No purpose-trained "pencil test" LoRA exists on CivitAI as of April 2026** — you will likely need to train one (~100–200 frames; see civitai.com/articles/20389). Known Kijai-wrapper quirk (Issue #1837): output strips the supplied last frame (`images[:, 0:-1]`) — not an issue with the native node.

### 2. LTX-Video 0.9.7 / 0.9.8 13B distilled (FP8)

**Overview.** Lightricks' DiT-based family, the speed leader among open-weights. LTXV 0.9.5 (March 5, 2025) introduced **native multi-keyframe conditioning (first-last frame, arbitrary waypoints)** via `LTXVKeyFrameConditioning` — the first open model where FLF2V was a first-class core feature rather than a fork. LTXV 0.9.7 (May 2025) was the first **13 B** variant with 30× speedup; 0.9.7-distilled-fp8 hits real-time-ish generation on a 4090. 0.9.8-dev (July 2025) added IC-LoRA Union control (depth+edges). LTX-2 (Jan 2026, 19B) added 4 K/50 fps/20 s and native joint audio, integrated into ComfyUI core v0.8.0 on Jan 7, 2026. OpenRail-M license (commercial-friendly under ~$10M revenue). Repos: github.com/Lightricks/LTX-Video, github.com/Lightricks/ComfyUI-LTXVideo.

**Quality vs Veo 3.1 / Kling 2.x / Seedance 2.0.** On the Apr 2026 Artificial Analysis I2V arena (no audio), **LTX-2 Pro is the #1 open-weights model at Elo 1,196** — ahead of HunyuanVideo 1.5 (1,121) and Wan 2.2 A14B (1,119), but still **~50 Elo behind Veo 3.1, ~100 Elo behind Kling 3.0, and ~156 Elo behind Seedance 2.0**. With audio, LTX-2.3 Fast leads open-weights at Elo 968. Strengths: **30×+ faster than Hunyuan and 3–5× faster than Wan 2.2** on the same 4090 (Apatero benchmarks: "LTX-2 generates 5-second 768×512 video in 45–90 seconds on an RTX 4090" vs Wan 2.2 at 3–6 minutes). Weaknesses: the Medium showdown by Lada Hang flagged "speed demon that melts human faces"; MimicPC's head-to-head said *"LTXV's output collapses — character details like faces and hands vanish entirely, making it the least reliable"* for complex human motion.

**Community reception.** (a) HF discussion huggingface.co/Lightricks/LTX-Video/discussions/6: multiple users reporting 4090 real-time-ish generation ("121 frames in 11 seconds") and sub-10 GB VRAM configurations. (b) Jukka Seppänen (Kijai), per PRNewswire announcement: *"LTXV transforms a single image into a dynamic video with prompt-based control."* (c) GitHub Issue Lightricks/ComfyUI-LTXVideo#309 (open): community still requesting an official packaged LTX-2.3 FLF2V template — for now, use **LTXV 0.9.7/0.9.8 FLF2V**, which is rock-solid and shipping as a core ComfyUI template.

**ComfyUI setup.** Install github.com/Lightricks/ComfyUI-LTXVideo (2.6k⭐). Download `ltxv-13b-0.9.7-distilled-fp8.safetensors` (huggingface.co/Lightricks/LTX-Video-0.9.7-distilled) or 0.9.8 equivalent. Multi-keyframe workflow JSONs bundled in `custom_nodes/ComfyUI-LTXVideo/example_workflows/`; ComfyUI-native multiscale first/last-frame workflow documented at blog.comfy.org/p/ltx-video-095-day-1-support-in-comfyui and comfyanonymous.github.io/ComfyUI_examples/ltxv/. For LTX-2.3 FLF2V today, use Kijai's or RunComfy's community template (runcomfy.com/comfyui-workflows/ltx-2-3-first-last-frame-in-comfyui).

**Hardware reality.** 13B distilled-fp8 fits 24 GB easily; multiscale pipeline upscales a low-res base pass (768×512 or 512×768) to 1280×720. Community confirms 6–8 GB setups work on the 2B model. Full LTX-2 19B at 22B params is **tight on 24 GB** — use FP8/NVFP4 quants or the distilled variant; a 48 GB 4090 is ideal but not required. **Mac Silicon: officially supported** — LTX-Video is one of the only serious open-weights video models with sanctioned MPS support (github.com/Lightricks/LTX-Video, tested with PyTorch 2.3/2.6). An M1 MBP 16 GB takes ~15 min per clip; MLX ports exist (github.com/james-see/ltx-video-mac, github.com/Acelogic/LTX-2-MLX). This alone makes LTXV your de-facto MacBook fallback.

**I2V with end-frame support.** ✅ **Native since 0.9.5** — `LTXVKeyFrameConditioning`, `LTXVSequenceConditioning`, `LTXVAddGuide` nodes accept arbitrary waypoint frames with `-1` denoting "last frame." Multi-keyframe (more than two) also supported natively. LTX-2.3 FLF2V is community-workflow only as of April 2026 (Issue #309).

**Known limitations.** Face/hand motion artifacts on complex human shots. Fewer pencil/anime LoRAs than Wan or Hunyuan, though training is well-supported (official LTX-Video-Trainer repo, Control LoRAs for depth/pose/canny). Stylization proof-of-concept exists (huggingface.co/valiantcat/LTX-2.3-Transition-LORA demonstrates hand-drawn comic and anime restyles). For pencil-test specifically, **expect to train a small style LoRA** using 20–50 of your own frames.

### 3. AnimateDiff-Evolved + Banodoco Steerable Motion

**Overview.** Yes, AnimateDiff is still in the top 5 — **specifically because it's not a dedicated video model**. It's a temporal motion module that rides on any SD 1.5 or SDXL checkpoint and LoRA stack. For your use case, this is the feature, not a bug: your spatial layers are *your* pencil-test checkpoint (an Illustrious/NoobAI base with a pencil LoRA, or a DreamBooth fine-tune of your own drawings), so the aesthetic is preserved by definition rather than negotiated with a photoreal prior. The **Banodoco Steerable Motion** pipeline (github.com/banodoco/Steerable-Motion) combines IPAdapter-Plus (anchor both keyframes) + SparseCtrl (inject image conditioning at frames 0 and N) + Lineart ControlNet (lock construction lines) to produce true start+end frame travel. ComfyUI-AnimateDiff-Evolved repo: github.com/Kosinkadink/ComfyUI-AnimateDiff-Evolved (3.4k⭐).

**Quality vs Veo 3.1 / Kling 2.x / Seedance 2.0.** AnimateDiff **loses badly** against all three on photoreal motion, physics, and cinematic quality — jittery motion is its signature weakness. But on your **actual metric — preserving cross-hatched pencil lines on cream paper** — it wins decisively because none of the frontier closed models respects rough-animation aesthetic. Veo 3.1 will clean your pencil frames toward polished illustration; Kling 2.x will smooth hatching into flat cel shading; Seedance 2.0 will frequently resynthesize the subject entirely in a photoreal key. The InsiderLLM 2026 summary is direct: *"Best for: Stylized animations, leveraging existing SD checkpoints and LoRAs. Motion can look jittery or looping rather than cinematic. The technique is showing its age against purpose-built video diffusion models."*

**Community reception.** (a) Banodoco on X (2025-05-18): *"VACE for Wan 14b is able to transfer complex motion while retaining exceptional character consistency — with very minor distortion"* — Banodoco themselves have moved some motion work to Wan/VACE, but **keep Steerable Motion's AnimateDiff pipeline alongside** as the alternative approach, not a deprecation. (b) yosefk.com's longform survey of hand-drawn interpolation concluded that non-generative flow interpolators (RIFE, GMFSS) fail on non-linear motion, ToonCrafter handles cartoon-domain but has a quality ceiling, and generative video diffusion models drift stylistically — positioning AnimateDiff+SparseCtrl as the practical middle ground. (c) The ACM SIGGRAPH 2024 paper "Generative AI for 2D Character Animation" (arxiv.org/html/2405.11098v2) used AnimateDiff + ControlNet with a DreamBooth-fine-tuned SDXL on hand-drawn keyframes rather than any DiT video model **because the DiT models all over-smooth pencil line weight**.

**ComfyUI setup.** Install Kosinkadink/ComfyUI-AnimateDiff-Evolved, Kosinkadink/ComfyUI-Advanced-ControlNet, cubiq/ComfyUI_IPAdapter_plus, FizzNodes, Fannovel16/comfyui_controlnet_aux, Fannovel16/ComfyUI-Frame-Interpolation. Clone github.com/banodoco/Steerable-Motion and load the "Smooth n' Steady" preset. Use mm_sd_v15_v3.ckpt motion module and an SD1.5 pencil/illustration checkpoint. For 24 fps / 720p output: render at 512–768 × 16 frames, upscale each frame (4x-AnimeSharp), then 2× RIFE temporal interpolation.

**Hardware reality.** **Trivial on a 24 GB 4090** — typical runs peak at 8–14 GB. The biggest constraint is iteration discipline, not VRAM. **Mac Silicon: works** — AnimateDiff-Evolved explicitly supports M1/M2/M3 per its README, though slower. This is actually the most Mac-friendly option alongside LTX-Video. **FramePack-F1's GitHub Discussion #459** noted one big missing piece — *"What I find myself missing is the ability to add LORAs"* — which is exactly AnimateDiff's home turf.

**I2V with end-frame support.** ✅ Via IPAdapter (identity anchors) + SparseCtrl (keyframe injection at frame 0 and frame N). Banodoco ships five preset workflows including "Smooth n' Steady" (smooth motion between images) and "Rad Attack" (more dynamic). Dough (github.com/banodoco/Dough) wraps it in a GUI if the graph gets unwieldy.

**Known limitations.** Max reliable clip length is ~2–4 seconds before motion drift; longer clips need chained context windows. Motion is less "cinematic" than Wan/Hunyuan/LTXV (it's motion module inference, not joint spatiotemporal training). SDXL motion modules exist but the community consensus is they produce worse motion than SD1.5 — stick with SD1.5 + upscale. Kosinkadink's repo is lightly maintained in 2025–2026 (last README update Mar 13 2025); an `AppMana/appmana-comfyui-nodes-animatediff-evolved` fork exists but uptake is small.

### 4. Wan 2.1 FLF2V-14B-720P

**Overview.** Released April 17, 2025 (huggingface.co/Wan-AI/Wan2.1-FLF2V-14B-720P, 227 likes, 53.9k downloads, Apache 2.0, arxiv.org/abs/2503.20314). **Purpose-built FLF2V weights** — the only open model with dedicated first-and-last-frame training rather than a node re-wiring. Official README claim (take with skepticism — self-reported): "matching rate of first and last frames reaches 98%; video jitter rate reduced by 37%." Trained primarily on Chinese text-video pairs, so **Chinese prompts yield better results** according to the README.

**Quality vs Veo 3.1 / Kling 2.x / Seedance 2.0.** Worse than Wan 2.2 on raw motion quality, but **different motion prior** — some users report Wan 2.1 FLF2V feels more "animated"/less cinematically polished, which can be an asset for rough animation. The Wavespeed I2V comparison explicitly calls out that the Wan family's first/last frame control is "the most feature-rich option... giving you more levers to pull" vs Seedance/Sora/Veo. Falls short of Veo/Kling/Seedance on photoreal motion by a wider margin than Wan 2.2.

**Community reception.** (a) Wan2GP changelog (April 18, 2025): *"FLF2V model support, official support from Wan for image2video start and end frames specialized for 720p"* — enabled sub-10 GB VRAM inference via deepbeepmeep/Wan2GP. (b) GitHub Issue kijai/ComfyUI-WanVideoWrapper#1183 (aminesoulaymani): *"I used to generate Wan 2.1 (First Last Frame to Video 720p (FLF2V) 14B) with SageAttention 2 + Compile Transformer Model, on a old laptop with RTX 3060 6GB VRAM. that's was slow but no OOM's"* — evidence FLF2V functions even at 6 GB with Wan2GP. (c) Civitai 1450534 model card for Wan 2.1-Fun-1.3B-InP: *"It was trained for start and end frame inpainting. Setting just a start frame allows it to do i2v"* — confirms Fun-InP as sibling FLF2V path.

**ComfyUI setup.** Native `WanFirstLastFrameToVideo` node (docs.comfy.org/tutorials/video/wan/wan-flf). Kijai wrapper sample JSON: github.com/kijai/ComfyUI-WanVideoWrapper/blob/main/example_workflows/wanvideo_FLF2V_720P_example_02.json (toggle `fun_or_fl2v_model` to true). Quantized weights: huggingface.co/Kijai/WanVideo_comfy (FP8) and huggingface.co/city96/Wan2.1-FLF2V-14B-720P-gguf (Q3–Q8).

**Hardware reality.** FP8 ~16–17 GB, GGUF Q5_K_S ~12 GB — all comfortable on a 4090. No Mac MPS support officially; HighDoping/Wan2.1-Mac includes FLF2V but runs at 20–100× slower than CUDA.

**I2V with end-frame support.** ✅ Native, purpose-trained. This is its whole reason for existing.

**Known limitations.** Kijai wrapper strips the supplied last frame from the output (Issue #1837 — `images[:, 0:-1]`); native node does not. Chinese-prompt bias in training data. Superseded on raw quality by Wan 2.2's I2V-A14B re-wired through the FLF2V node, but still worth A/B testing because the motion prior is different.

### 5. HunyuanVideo 1.5 (distilled I2V) / HunyuanVideo-I2V 13B + Dashtoon Keyframe LoRA

**Overview.** Tencent's family — HunyuanVideo T2V shipped December 2024 (13B, 129 frames @ 24 fps, 720p); HunyuanVideo-I2V shipped March 2025 (first-frame only, no end-frame); **HunyuanVideo 1.5 released November 20, 2025** as an 8.3B efficient successor with step-distilled I2V (8/12 steps, ~75 s per 5 s clip at 480p on a 4090). Repos: github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HunyuanVideo-1.5 and github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HunyuanVideo-I2V. License: Hunyuan Community License (not strictly open-source, but permissive for non-competing commercial use).

**Quality vs Veo 3.1 / Kling 2.x / Seedance 2.0.** HunyuanVideo 1.5 sits at **Elo 1,121** on Artificial Analysis I2V (April 2026), roughly matching Wan 2.2 A14B (1,119) and trailing LTX-2 Pro (1,196) among open-weights — all well behind the proprietary leaders. VBench-2.0 paper (arxiv.org/html/2503.21755v2): *"HunyuanVideo is relatively weaker in many VBench-2.0 dimensions [but] demonstrates impressive strengths in human-related aspects (Human Fidelity and Motion Rationality)."* The HunyuanVideo 1.5 tech report (arxiv.org/html/2511.18870v1) claims SOTA among open-source vs Kling 2.5/Veo 3.1/Sora 2 but acknowledges it does not beat closed models. **Human motion is Hunyuan's actual strength** — if your pencil tests have characters/figures as the focus, this model outperforms Wan on anatomy and gesture.

**Community reception.** (a) HuggingFace discussion huggingface.co/city96/HunyuanVideo-I2V-gguf/discussions/3: *"With Kijai's model, I can generate the 129 frames, and come in just under 24GB of VRAM on my 4090 (96%) at 720x720 with 20 steps."* Confirms 24 GB viability at 720². (b) Civitai 1084814 (Studio Ghibli LoRA author seruva19): *"Although base HunyuanVideo knows generic anime style well without need for LoRAs and has some knowledge about Studio Ghibli's art style, the latter is not consistent, very prompt-dependant and can sometimes fall back to realistic style."* Same author later: *"After testing Wan2.1-14B-T2V for a week, I must acknowledge that it is superior to HV."* (c) CrePal Mar 2026 review: *"The 12-step distilled [HunyuanVideo 1.5] model is my daily driver… Hunyuan occasionally jumped from close-up to distant shot mid-clip — jarring."*

**ComfyUI setup.** Native `HunyuanImageToVideo` node (blog.comfy.org/p/hunyuan-image2video-day-1-support). Kijai wrapper github.com/kijai/ComfyUI-HunyuanVideoWrapper (2.4k⭐) has fp8 loader, LoRA loader, block-swap, TeaCache. GGUF via huggingface.co/city96/HunyuanVideo-I2V-gguf + city96/ComfyUI-GGUF. **For FLF2V specifically, use dashtoon/hunyuan-video-keyframe-control-lora** — a LoRA adapter on HunyuanVideo **T2V** (not I2V) that doubles patch-embed `in_chans` to condition on start+end frames; pftq/Hunyuan_Keyframe_Lite is the community-polished implementation with sage/flash attention.

**Hardware reality.** Official FP16 needs 60 GB for 720p. **FP8 (Kijai) or GGUF Q8 runs 720×720 × 129 frames at ~96% of 24 GB** — tight but viable. Full 1280×720 at 129 frames is a gamble (OOM risk on V2 weights; V1 more stable). HunyuanVideo 1.5 distilled is comfortable at 480p × 5 s in ~75 s. **Mac Silicon: not supported in any practical sense** — the HF discussion on MPS concluded it would take *"one day to generate a single video"* even with 90 GB+ RAM.

**I2V with end-frame support.** ⚠️ **Start-frame only in official code. FLF2V exists only via Dashtoon's keyframe-control LoRA (on T2V-13B, not I2V, not 1.5)** — more fragile setup than Wan 2.2, and HunyuanVideo 1.5 does not yet have an equivalent keyframe LoRA. If end-frame control is non-negotiable, Wan 2.2 is the safer pick; use Hunyuan as a secondary for single-image I2V human shots.

**Known limitations.** Photoreal bias stronger than Wan; "falls back to realistic style" even with anime LoRAs (seruva19's admission). No official ControlNet. No native MPS. License is permissive but not OSI-approved open-source. Pencil-test preservation requires a custom-trained LoRA — diffusion-pipe and musubi-tuner both support this on 24 GB.

## Section 3 — Stylized / 2D animation subnote

This is the one section where the ranking genuinely flips. For your portfolio piece, **the ability to preserve cream-paper cross-hatching and construction lines matters more than raw motion quality** — and here, purpose-built video DiTs are actively worse than the older AnimateDiff/ToonCrafter stack.

**Which models respect stylized input, ranked.** **ToonCrafter** (Tencent/CUHK, 2024) is literally the only open model trained on 2D cartoon domain; its paper explicitly addresses the "lack of motion blur in cartoon animations" problem that plagues live-action-trained models. Cited as the cartoon-domain baseline in the 2025 VFIBench paper (arxiv.org/html/2501.03699v1). Weakness: 16 frames × 512×320 hard cap, aging since May 2024. **AnimateDiff-Evolved + Banodoco Steerable Motion** (SD1.5 base) preserves pencil aesthetic by definition because the spatial layers doing the drawing are your chosen checkpoint. **Wan 2.2** is workable **only with a matching anime/stylized LoRA plus aggressive negative prompting** — the author of civitai.com/models/2222779 states it outright: *"WAN 2.2 appears to have a strong bias towards realistic generations… more consistent generation can be achieved by bumping the model strength up to 1.2 or adding `((realistic))` to your negative prompt."* **CogVideoX-Fun-InP** is middling — stylization-capable but 8 fps output looks choppy. **Hunyuan, FramePack, LTXV, Mochi, Open-Sora, Step-Video all lean photoreal** — FramePack especially because it inherits Hunyuan's live-action prior; MimicPC flagged LTXV as outright collapsing on stylized input ("character details like faces and hands vanish entirely").

**The practitioner consensus is a hybrid pipeline.** Generate pencil start/end frames in SDXL/Illustrious/NoobAI with pencil + cream-paper LoRAs (or scan real drawings). Then pick between three motion paths: **(A)** AnimateDiff-Evolved + Steerable Motion with Lineart ControlNet locking construction lines — highest aesthetic fidelity, ~2–4 s clips, requires SD1.5 downport; **(B)** ToonCrafter for the 16-frame interpolation pass at 512×320, then FlashVSR (via Kijai's wrapper) to upscale and RIFE to 24 fps — highest style preservation but short clip length; **(C)** Wan 2.2 FLF2V + Anime Style WAN 2.2 I2V LoRA (civitai 2222779) at strength 1.2 + Lightning 4-step LoRA — longest clips, most modern, budget a cleanup pass because hatching will partially smooth out. **Do not use FramePack for this** despite its low-VRAM appeal — the paper's own community discussion (Discussion #459) confirms its Hunyuan-based prior is the wrong shape for rough pencil animation.

**Open gap in the ecosystem.** No dedicated "pencil test," "rough animation," or "construction line" LoRA exists on CivitAI for any video model as of April 2026 — the closest are colored-pencil illustration LoRAs (civitai 1155749 "Hyperdetailed Colored Pencil — Wan2.2") and generic anime LoRAs. **Plan to train a small pencil-test LoRA** on ~100–200 of your own frames using diffusion-pipe or musubi-tuner for Wan/Hunyuan, or LTX-Video-Trainer for LTXV. The ACM SIGGRAPH 2024 paper "Generative AI for 2D Character Animation" used exactly this pattern: DreamBooth-fine-tuned SDXL + AnimateDiff + ControlNet on hand-drawn keyframes — not a DiT video model. The ACM 2025 hand-painted watercolor short "Sensual" used RIFE/optical-flow interpolation in Nuke rather than any generative model specifically **because generative video models resynthesize pixels and drift stylistically** — worth considering as a post-interpolation step on any pipeline above.

## Section 4 — What's emerging

**SkyReels-V3** (Skywork AI, **January 29, 2026**, github.com/SkyworkAI/SkyReels-V3, HF Skywork/SkyReels-V3-R2V-14B + V3-A2V-19B): unified ref-image-to-video + V2V extension + audio-to-video. If you have patience for community workflow iteration, this is the most promising next-step upgrade path from Wan 2.2. **SkyReels V4** (February 25, 2026, arxiv 2602.21818) sits at Elo 1,289 on the I2V arena — ahead of Veo 3.1 proper — but currently API/preview only; a V3-style weights release is plausible in coming weeks based on Skywork's cadence.

**HunyuanVideo 1.5** step-distilled I2V and FP8 GEMM updates are still landing (step-distilled I2V December 5, 2025; FP8 GEMM December 23, 2025). Official ComfyUI plugin maturing. No FLF2V variant shipped yet, and no clear roadmap for one.

**LTX-2.3** (March 2026, Lightricks/LTX-2.3 on HF, 22B distilled). Desktop app shipped. FLF2V via community workflows only (Issue #309 tracks official packaging). Audio-native. Monitor for a first-class FLF2V template in Q2 2026 — when that lands, LTXV likely becomes the default pick on speed + quality.

## Conclusion: what to actually build this week

Start with **Wan 2.2 FLF2V via the native ComfyUI node**, FP8-scaled weights, Lightning 4-step LoRA v2, plus a stacked anime/stylized LoRA at ~1.2 strength and hard negative prompts against realism. This gets you to 5 s @ 720p @ 16 fps in 3–6 minutes per clip with genuine end-frame control. If your pencil hatching survives the pass with cleanup, you're done — this is the closest you'll get to a drop-in Veo/Kling/Seedance replacement for your specific aesthetic. **Pay attention to two uncertainties: (a) Wan 2.2's photoreal bias is real and style-LoRA strength will need tuning per shot; (b) no one has publicly stress-tested Wan 2.2 FLF2V on pencil-test input, so your first A/B test is also novel community data.**

If Wan 2.2 flattens your hatching beyond repair, drop to the **AnimateDiff-Evolved + Banodoco Steerable Motion (SD1.5 + Lineart ControlNet + SparseCtrl + IPAdapter)** pipeline — older, slower, 2–4 s clips, but it's the only approach where the aesthetic-preservation guarantee is architectural rather than negotiated. For long clips, **LTX-Video 0.9.8 13B distilled** is your speed/iteration tool. Keep **ToonCrafter** in your back pocket for shots where style fidelity matters more than length. Invest a weekend into training a small pencil-test LoRA on your own frames (diffusion-pipe for Wan, musubi-tuner for Hunyuan, LTX-Video-Trainer for LTXV) — this is where the compounding win lives, and it's the missing piece no one in the public LoRA ecosystem has shipped yet.

The honest bottom line: **you can leave Veo/Kling/Seedance** for this project — but the reason you can is that your aesthetic is their weakness, not that open has closed the photoreal gap. Keep a small API budget for any hero photoreal reference shots you need; do everything pencil locally.
