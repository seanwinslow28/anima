*Case-design discipline (from the findings doc §2 — apply it, don't improvise):*

- **Build cases by open-coding real frames, not by listing failure modes in advance.** Read the real Act 1 / Act 2 / Cy-run frames first, write open-ended notes on what's actually wrong in each, and let the failure taxonomy *emerge* from the data (the `failure-modes.md` is the axial-coding output, written *after* you've looked, not before). This is the error-analysis-first discipline that the vault-synthesizer suite already embodies — its six modes came from 17 days of real logs, not imagination.
- **Count: a few dozen, not 200.** The brainstorm's "200-frame defect set" is an aspiration, not a v1 gate. Best practice for a starting suite is 20–50 cases drawn from real failures (Anthropic); at solo scale, **30–60 cases is the honest v1**, growing as the fleet matures. Don't delay the baseline waiting for 200.
- **Class balance is non-negotiable.** Include cases where a defect *should* be flagged **and** cases where a clean frame *should* pass. "One-sided evals create one-sided optimization" — an all-defects suite rewards a critic that flags everything. Aim for a roughly balanced split, and record the balance in `cases.yaml` comments.
- **Sean is the single benevolent-dictator labeler.** No inter-rater-reliability machinery needed at solo scale (Cohen's κ only matters with a second labeler — note it as a "later, if ever" in `failure-modes.md`, don't build it). Label binary-plus-critique: the expected verdict *and* a one-line note on *why*, so the rubric is externalized.
- **Handle `borderline` deliberately.** Three-way verdicts reintroduce the boundary ambiguity binary was meant to kill. Em's `cites_criteria` invariant is what keeps `borderline` honest (a citation is forced). In the suite, also score "should this `borderline` have been a `fail`?" as its own question — the false-pass danger (§ below) lives in the borderline→pass slippage.
- **The baseline is a SCORED CONFUSION MATRIX, not a smoke trace.** Run Em across the set and report **precision and recall on the defect class, plus the false-pass rate** — *not* raw agreement (it misleads under the class imbalance real defect data carries) and *not* F1 alone (it hides true negatives, i.e. the false-pass blind spot). This baseline *is* the judge-vs-Sean's-labels calibration measurement that Phase 2 builds on — capturing it correctly here means Phase 2 inherits a real calibration baseline, not a re-do.
- **A disagreement between Em and Sean's label is a fork, not a bug.** It's either a judge-calibration problem (tighten Em's prompt) or a criteria-drift discovery (Sean's rubric sharpened by the act of grading — legitimate per Shankar's "you can't fully specify criteria before grading"). Distinguishing the two is the work. **Do not tune a case so Em passes it** — that's measuring the thermometer against itself (Goodhart). Editing a case is legitimate only when the *case or expected-verdict was wrong* (a validity fix), never to make Em look better.

*The decisive finding — read findings doc §3.5 before you build this, because it changes what the contact sheet can honestly claim:* **multimodal LLMs frequently answer video questions correctly even when the frames are shuffled** (arXiv 2505.14321). They read static content and ignore temporal order. So a contact-sheet judge that *appears* to assess motion is very likely not seeing time at all. The consequences for this phase are concrete and must be honored — building the contact sheet and claiming motion coverage would be the trap this whole session exists to avoid:

- **The contact sheet is the RIGHT tool for what's actually content-across-time:** identity drift (does Sean stay Sean frame-to-frame), style/aesthetic drift (does the line weight / paper grain / register hold), stylus-hand continuity, outfit consistency. These are the failure modes Em's lens already names, and the "Image Grid Can Be Worth a Video" work (arXiv 2403.18406) shows VLMs handle them zero-shot. **Label the Phase 0 motion cases for these, and expect the contact sheet to catch them.**
- **The contact sheet is the WRONG tool for true motion quality:** motion-arc smoothness, jitter/skating, temporal flicker, texture-crawl/boiling. Do **not** expect Em-on-contact-sheet to catch these, and do **not** label the suite as if she should. The honest options for those, in order of cheapness: **(a)** a deterministic **warping-error / E_warp** check (optical-flow frame-to-frame residual — cheap, targets flicker/boiling directly); **(b)** VBench's **motion-smoothness + temporal-flickering + dynamic-degree** as a *set* — and you **must** include a dynamism axis or you reward a frozen clip (VBench documents the consistency↔dynamic-degree trade-off where a near-static video trivially maximizes "consistency"); **(c)** a human takes the look. **Avoid FVD** for temporal artifacts — it's biased toward per-frame image quality and is nearly blind to temporal corruption. For motion-arc *consistency* specifically, FVMD (arXiv 2407.16124) is the targeted metric.
- **Back identity drift with DINOv2, not the MLLM.** Em-on-contact-sheet can *opine* on identity, but the reliable identity signal across the clip is **DINOv2 cosine vs a per-view anchor** — anima already has the similarity_gate ladder. Use it as the deterministic backstop; the MLLM is the prose layer over it. (And per §3.5, prefer **pairwise** framing where you can — "is frame A or frame B closer to the anchor" — since MLLM judges are far more reliable pairwise than on absolute scores.)
- **The pencil-test wrinkle:** flicker-minimizing metrics will *fight* the intentional line "boil" that's part of the hand-drawn aesthetic. Distinguishing stylistic boil (wanted) from artifact boil (unwanted) is genuinely hard for automated metrics — flag it as a human-look candidate, don't try to make a deterministic gate adjudicate it.

So the plan's real design fork is sharper than "contact sheet vs video input": it's **"contact sheet for identity/style-across-time (build it), plus an explicit decision on how motion-proper gets covered (deterministic metric vs honest human seam)."** State that decision in the plan. A motion critic that *looks honest about what it can't see* is worth more than one that silently passes shuffled-frame guesses as motion verdicts.
