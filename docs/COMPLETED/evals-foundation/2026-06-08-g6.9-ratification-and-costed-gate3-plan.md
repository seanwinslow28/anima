3. **The run.** `patch_efficacy` — but **add a null/placebo arm** to the existing `--arm {em,golden,both}` (the same clean-pair mechanism with a deliberately-irrelevant clause). Attribution = **normalized lift `(em − null)/(golden − null)`**, with raw null/em/golden clear-rate bands printed **per class** (the mechanism's baseline differs by class — a palette clause spliced into a clean base almost always clears; anatomy is more clause-sensitive). Rationale: the dual arms share the clean-pair confound so em-vs-golden cancels most of it, but only the null arm reveals the *floor* — if `null ≈ golden` on a class, the test has no discriminative power there and that's a measured finding, not a guess. Suggested first run: `--arm both+null --sample 12 --rerolls 3` (~+50% gen vs the 2-arm $5–6 → ≈ $8–9; the estimate prints before spend). Periodic, not per-run; later runs may drop the null arm once the floor is characterized.
4. **Attribution caveat note.** Document that the clean-pair mechanism (chosen for robustness across the three defect grammars) carries a baseline-clearing artifact; the null arm measures it, the golden control bounds it, identity-held keeps it honest. View class is exempt (no-regen, label-side).

**Out of scope for the run:** switching the apply mechanism to full-prompt regen (re-opens the three-grammar problem; revisit only if the null arm shows the instrument can't discriminate) · auto-applying patches · moving the verdict baseline.
